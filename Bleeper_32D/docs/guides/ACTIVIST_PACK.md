# Starbeam Activist Pack

This guide is for groups who need resilient, low profile communications in high risk or disrupted environments.

**Use cases**
- Community safety teams and mutual aid networks.
- Event coordination when cell service is unreliable.
- Rapid response teams in disasters.

**Risk first approach**
- Assume devices can be lost or inspected.
- Use passkeys and rotate them per event.
- Use minimal, plain language messages.

**Operational planning**
- Assign a comms lead and a backup lead.
- Map hub locations and alternate safe points.
- Define message formats before deployment.

**Message formats**
- Use a short prefix, a location, and an action.
- Example: SAFE OAK ST NEED WATER
- Example: MED 5TH AVE NEED KIT

**Deployment checklist**
- Test all nodes the day before.
- Pack spare power and one backup ESP32.
- Confirm each role has a device and training.

**Privacy and safety**
- Avoid names unless essential.
- Do not send sensitive personal data.
- Avoid broadcasting exact locations unless urgent.

**De escalation**
- Cancel emergency broadcasts once resolved.
- Log incidents for internal review.
- Rotate passkeys after each action.

**After action notes**
- Review coverage gaps and add nodes next time.
- Reflash devices if you suspect compromise.
- Update training based on what worked.
